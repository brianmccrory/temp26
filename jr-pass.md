# JR Pass
date: 2023-11-02

If you really want to get the most out of travel within Japan, there's a Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass) system, where you can buy a "all you can ride" ticket which is valid for 7, 14, or some number of days (depending on ticket) and greatly simplifies things for foreign travelers. But this isn't worth it unless you want to go to at least two different cities other than Tokyo, spending more time on bullet trains and seeing more parts of the country. I used to buy a JR Rail Pass every time I went to Japan and it was really useful. These days I mostly stay in Tokyo and don't explore other cities as much as I used to.

This pass is only offered to foreign visitors, and (in the past) you need to purchase the ticket online or through at an official office, pick up the ticket/voucher, and exchange it for the actual pass once you get to Japan (it may be different or be easier now). In other words, you couldn't suddenly decide to buy the pass once you get to Japan (this seems to be easier now, and some passes can be bought in Japan).

For people without a JR Pass, to go to a different city, you can just buy a point-to-point ticket on a specific train or bullet train, like buying airline tickets.
