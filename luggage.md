# Luggage
date: 2023-11-13

If possible, take a carry-on size luggage and a backpack/bag, and avoid taking a large, check-in size luggage. 

Taking a large suitcase (larger than airplane carry-on size) will be more trouble that it's worth:

* Bullet trains don't have extra space for large suitcases. You may have to keep it in the end space of the train, away from your seat, if it doesn't fit in the overhead luggage rack. 
* Unless you plan to take taxis everywhere, you will be using train and subway stations (moreso if you plan to leave Tokyo and visit other cities during your trip). Many of them have escalators and elevators, but sometimes the elevators are hard to locate or out of the way, and some stations only have stairs. Carrying heavy, large suitcases up and down station staircases is really not fun.
* Local trains and subways can get extremely crowded at certain times, and large suitcases can be an obstacle and hinderance when trying to get on/off the trains at crowded times. Also, no storage space for large suitcases, so they will have to be kept right next to you. 
* The larger the suitcase, the heavier it gets (with more things packed inside). There is a lot of walking, stairs, elevators, and escalators when traveling between the airport, stations, and the hotels (unless you are preparing to splurge on taxis everywhere).
* Standard hotel rooms are quite small. With a large suitcase, there will likely be very little space for you to keep the suitcase out of your way -- even worse if two people have two large suitcases in the same a room.
