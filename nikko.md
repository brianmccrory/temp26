# Nikko
date: 2023-11-07

[Nikko](https://www.jrailpass.com/blog/nikko-itinerary) is a city and national park north of Tokyo. It takes around 2-3 hours to get there by train, 2 hours by car, or 5 hours by bus. I've visited Nikko twice, once in July 2005 (1 night) and once in September 2017 (2 nights).

Nikko is popular for visitors to Japan. There are great must-visit [shrine and temple sites](https://www.japan.travel/en/world-heritage/the-shrines-and-temples-of-nikko/), hiking and onsens, and it's relatively close to Tokyo (and great for JR Pass holders with "all you can ride" JR train benefits).

Nikko can be visited as a day trip from Tokyu, but it's better to stay at least one night.

The "Dragon's Canyon" hiking path ([Ryuokyo hiking](https://en.japantravel.com/tochigi/kinugawa-ryuokyo-hiking/4550)) is excellent.

The [Tobu World Square and architectural theme park](https://en.tobuws.co.jp/) is interesting (although only if you have extra hours in Nikko). It's an outdoor park filled with scale-model replicas of world monuments, famous buildings, and world heritage sites all built to 1:25 scale (like large dollhouses), accurately recreated.
