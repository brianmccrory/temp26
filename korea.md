# Korea
date: 2023-11-06

I've been to South Korea three times as quick trips from Japan.

I've been to Busan once (July 2014) and Seoul twice (Jan 2015 and June 2016). 

Seoul is towards the north and feels like a sprawling, active city, kind of like Tokyo or Osaka, but somehow more wide-spread, a little more old-fashioned/traditional in places and modern in other places, and maybe more casual or somewhat rougher around the edges, compared to Japan.

Busan is on the southern coast between mountains and the sea, a big city with a beach resort area, and feels like a major city surrounded by nature. This is the closest major South Korean city to southern Japan (Fukuoka city). 

You can take quick flights (about 2 hrs) from Tokyo to South Korea, or from other airports in Japan. There is also a ferry from southern Japan to Busan, which takes about 4 hours.

Eating is Korea is geared more towards groups, and it can be a little harder to find places to eat comfortably as a solo traveler. With groups, it's great to try bigger Korean family-style meals like korean barbeque and hotpot, with all the extra side dishes like kimchee and pickles that come with larger Korean meals.

I went to a few restaurants or bars where the menu is good for groups rather than individuals, and just ordered more food than I needed. Probably there are great places for solo diners as well, but I didn't happen across them (I probably should have used a guidebook). Compared to Japan, in Japan it is pretty easy for solo diners to grab a bite at many places simply, although you may be seated fairly close to your neighbors.

On the other hand, Korea has a ton of coffeeshops and sweets specialty shops (ice cream, shaved ice desserts) that are very popular, and these were great places for me to take a break, read a book, study, and eat - especially wnen I was in Korea on my own. Compared to Tokyo, cafes in Korea are larger and more spacious with many available seats.

There are also a ton of fried chicken places everywhere. Korean Fried Chicken (the other "KFC") is really good. You can also find bibimbap and spicy tofu soup at certain places, which is good and easy for a solo traveler.


I also found and visited several jazz bars in South Korea, where jazz is also popular.
