# Sightseeing Ideas Part 1
date: 2023-11-03

It's easy to get sightseeing ideas on the internet by just searching for 'japan sightseeing', 'tokyo sightseeing', 'japan tourist attractions', etc.

Some typical Tokyo ideas:
   * [Senso-ji temple](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3001.html) and the Asakusa neighborhood
   * [Tokyo Skytree](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3064.html)
   * [Tokyo Tower](https://www.kanpai-japan.com/tokyo/tower) - was more popular before Skytree, it's an Eiffel tower-like structure
   * [Akihabara](https://jw-webmagazine.com/things-to-do-in-akihabara/) aka "Electric Town", formerly popular for electronics buyers but these days very popular with anima/manga/cosplay/maid cafe-type tourism
   * Roppongi neighborhood for dance clubs, nightlife, and general rowdiness
   * Shibuya and Shibuya crossing for experiencing the famously crowded intersection with huge monitors and digital signs
   * Harajuku - young people, [cosplay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosplay), and trendy shopping and fashion area
   * Traditional Ueno park with museums, zoo, and temples
   * Yoyogi park with nearby [Meiji Jingu shrine and surrounding park](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e3002.html)
   * Tokyo Disneyland and Disney Sea
   * Ghibli Museum (for hardcore Studio Ghibli movie fans)
   * [Miraikan](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g1066449-d1456874-Reviews-National_Museum_of_Emerging_Science_and_Innovation_Miraikan-Koto_Tokyo_Tokyo_Pre.html) - National Museum of Emerging Science and Innovation, Japanese robots and such
   * Onsens - Spas and public baths
   * Karaoke rooms
   * Izakayas (bar and grill-type places)
   * Shopping in traditional Japanese department stores, or popular fast chainstores like [Muji](https://www.muji.com/jp/ja/store), [Loft](https://www.loft.co.jp/), [Tokyu Hands](https://info.hands.net/en/), [Uniqlo](https://www.uniqlo.com/jp/ja/).
   
Lots of people discuss & comment on their trip plans on [reddit /r/JapanTravel](https://www.reddit.com/r/JapanTravel) and other websites, with topics like "Japan Itinerary", "5-days Tokyo" etc... internet forums can be useful, but also filled with lots of mistakes, criticism, and bad opinions, so it can be interesting but don't take it as gospel.
