# Temples Shrines Castles Palaces
date: 2023-11-14

* **Temple:** Buddhist places of worship, names usually end with *ji*, *dera*, or *in*. Common features are pagodas, Buddha statues, large *sanmon* gate (three openings: emptiness, formlessness, no-action), gate guardians, monasteries, incense purification.
   * Risshakuji (Yamadera), Sazae-do (Aizu-Wakamatsu), Nanzenji, Eikando, Honen-in, Ginkaku-ji, Kinkaku-ji, Myoshin-ji, Ryoan-ji (Kyoto), Sensoji (Tokyo), Todai-ji (Nara)
* **Shrine:** Shinto places of worship, names usually end with *jingu*, *jinga*, or *taisha*. Common features are large *torii* gates, water purification, multiple gods, highlight nature, animal guardians.
   * Hie Shrine (Tokyo), Heian-jingu (Kyoto), Nagoya-jinja (Nagoya), Hanazono (Tokyo), Kokumai (Fukuoka), Itsukashima (Miyajima), Daizaifu Tenman-gu (Fukuoka), Yasukuni-jinja (Tokyo), Meiji-jingu (Tokyo), Kawagoe Hikawa Shrine (Kawagoe), Izumo Taisha (Izumo), Fujisan Hangusengen Shrine Okumiya and Kusushi Shrine (Mt. Fuji), Toshu-gu (Nikko), Tsurugaoka Hachimangu (Kamakura)
* **Castle:** Fortresses made of wood and stone, names usually end with *jo*.
   * Nagoya-ji (Nagoya), Osaka-jo (Osaka), Nijo-jo (Kyoto), Tsuraga-jo (Aizu-Wakamatsu), Hikone-jo (Hikone), **Matsue-jo** (Matsue), 
* **Palaces:** residence of Emperor of Japan. 
   * Tokyo Imperial Palace (formerly Edo Castle), Kyoto Imperial Palace (former residence).
