# 23 Wards of Tokyo
date: 2023-11-13

There are offically [twenty three wards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo) in the main part of the Tokyo metropolis (even more in the suburbs and neighboring cities):

   * Minato, Chiyoda, Chuo, Shinjuku, Bunkyo, Taito, Sumida, Koto, Shinagawa, Meguro, Ota, Setagaya, Shibuya, Nakano, Suginami, Toshima, Kita, Arakawa, Itabashi, Nerima, Adachi, Katsushika, Edogawa
   
First-time visitors and guidebooks will most likely concentrate on the following important districts within these wards, all easily connected by trains, buses, subways, taxis, or by walking:

   * Asakusa, Ueno (in Taito)
   * Akasaka, Roppongi, Odaiba (in Minato) 
   * Akihabara (in Chiyoda)
   * Shinjuku, Okubo, Takadanobaba (in Shinjuku)
   * Shibuya, Harajuku, Yoyogi (in Shibuya)
   * Ikebukuro (in Toshima)
   * Ginza, Tsukiji (in Chuo)
   * Shimokitazawa (in Setagaya)
   
One of the most useful trains in Tokyo is the [JR Yamanote Line](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2370.html), whose trains run in a huge loop in both directions, circling around 5 (Shinjuku, Shibuya, Minato, Chiyoda, Chuo) of the 23 wards in about an hour for a complete circuit, but providing good access to most of the other wards and various city centers.
